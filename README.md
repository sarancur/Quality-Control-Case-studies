## Overview

This repository contains a series of applied case studies examining the core pillars of Quality Control in modern organizations. Each study analyzes a specific QC framework or real-world system — moving from statistical process control and experimental design through to enterprise-grade audit and compliance infrastructure. The work reflects hands-on application of Lean Manufacturing principles and quality management theory to operational and supply chain contexts.

Topics covered: Statistical Process Control (SPC), Total Quality Management (TQM), Design of Experiments (DOE), Audit & Compliance Systems, Quality Certification, Quality Standards, and a named-company deep dive into Körber WMS.

## Case Studies

### 1. Körber WMS — Transforming Warehouse Audit & Compliance
**Focus:** Enterprise audit infrastructure, warehouse quality control, OTIF performance

The warehouse industry loses over $60 billion annually from inventory inaccuracies, mis-picks, and compliance failures — largely due to manual audit processes with no real-time visibility. This case study examines how Körber's Warehouse Management System (WMS) and its built-in Audit & Compliance Suite addresses four critical operational gaps:

- Manual audits causing inconsistencies and delays in quality reporting
- High mis-pick rates from inadequate validation checkpoints
- Cycle counting errors compromising inventory accuracy
- Lack of standardized compliance documentation across shifts

**Key frameworks analyzed:** Configurable audit templates for OSHA/ISO/SOP compliance, automated deviation-triggered audit workflows, root-cause analytics embedded in picking processes, real-time KPI dashboards tracking accuracy rates, cycle count variances, and pick errors.

**Quantified outcomes from 3PL use case:** An 18% improvement in OTIF (On-Time In-Full) performance within 3 months of Körber deployment, driven by real-time mis-pick validation and proactive error detection. Platform benchmarks: 40% inventory accuracy improvement, 20% labor reduction, 35% reduction in operational disruptions. Typical ROI window: 12–18 months.

**Competitive analysis:** Körber vs. legacy WMS audit solutions — evaluated on workflow integration depth, robotics compatibility, configuration flexibility, and analytics capability.

### 2. Statistical Process Control (SPC)
**Focus:** Control charts, process variation, defect detection

Examines the application of SPC methods to monitor and control manufacturing and operational processes. Covers control chart selection (X-bar, R-charts, P-charts), distinguishing common cause from assignable cause variation, and using SPC as an early-warning system for process drift before defects occur.

---

### 3. Total Quality Management (TQM)
**Focus:** Organization-wide quality culture, continuous improvement

Analyzes TQM as a management philosophy — covering customer-focus, process thinking, employee involvement, and fact-based decision making. Examines how TQM principles translate into measurable operational outcomes and how they interact with Lean and Six Sigma methodologies.

---

### 4. Design of Experiments (DOE)
**Focus:** Factorial design, variable optimization, process improvement

Covers the structured use of DOE to identify which process variables most significantly affect output quality. Includes full and fractional factorial designs, interaction effects, and the application of DOE findings to reduce variability and optimize process performance.

---

### 5. Audit Control
**Focus:** Internal audit frameworks, compliance monitoring, corrective action

Examines audit program design, audit cycle management, non-conformance identification, and the link between audit findings and corrective/preventive action (CAPA) systems. Connects audit control to ISO 9001 quality management requirements.

---

### 6. Quality Certification
**Focus:** ISO standards, certification pathways, audit readiness

Reviews the structure and requirements of quality certification frameworks (ISO 9001 and related standards), the certification audit process, and how organizations build and sustain audit-ready quality management systems.

---

### 7. Quality Standards
**Focus:** Industry quality standards, regulatory compliance, standardization

Analyzes how quality standards function as operational baselines — covering standard-setting bodies, standard implementation challenges, and the role of standardization in reducing defect rates and ensuring cross-functional consistency.
